01 — Positioning
Who, What, Why.

The core proposition framed for the program client (universities) and the participant (international students).

Target
International Students in Korea
  • Undergrad (3rd/4th yr) + grad students at Korean universities
  • Intent: launch or build something inside Korea
  • Often pre-team, pre-idea — but motivated
  • Also includes "in-between" students (Korean diaspora, heritage students)
  • Language: English program → Korean-speaking students encouraged to connect
Core Challenge
K-Market Localization Barrier
  • Language barrier in Korean-only startup education
  • Visa friction: D2 → working / startup visa paths unclear
  • No co-founder, no network, no "in" to the Korean startup ecosystem
  • Startup education at universities targets Korean students only
  • Employment is nearly impossible → entrepreneurship is the real alternative
Theme & Methodology
K-Market Entry for Foreign Founders
  • Reverse glocal posture — they are the "global" entering Korea
  • IMPACT methodology: I → M → P (Hackathon #1) → A (Hackathon #2)
  • CORE foundation: Act-preneur mindset, ACTT execution habits, 5D skillset
  • AI as co-founder: solo is valid, AI tools replace early team needs
  • Korean mentor pool + startup lawyer + alumni network as key resources

08 — Design Principles
Why It's Built This Way.
01
Short but Intense

Two focused 1N2D events are more realistic for students than a month-long curriculum. Students value their time — every block delivers a tangible output, not just knowledge.

02
Action Over Education

Every session ends with a deliverable. Underdogs' core principle: proof over promise. The program is designed for "Act-preneurs" — people who build first and learn by doing.

03
Visa Support as a Core Feature

Not an add-on. D2 → D10-2 → D8-4 is a critical pain point universities cannot solve alone. Including a startup lawyer makes this program uniquely valuable to both students and university administrators.

04
AI as Co-founder

Solo is valid. In an era where AI handles prototyping, writing, and strategy — one person with the right tools is a team. Claude + Lovable replace early co-founder dependencies.

05
Reverse Glocal Posture

International students ARE the global founders entering Korea. Their home-market knowledge + Korean ecosystem access creates a unique inbound ↔ outbound loop unavailable to Korean-only programs.

06
University Admin as Client, Student as User

The landing page talks to students. The proposal talks to universities. Universities want: participation numbers, satisfaction scores, visible tangible results, and reduced administrative burden (especially visa issues).
